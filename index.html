Reflective Essay

I once considered writing to be a utilitarian thing prior to this class a means for academics to convey ideas and get work done, that's all. But over the course of the semester, I've developed a much greater respect for writing as a craft, especially in technical communication. Now, I view technical writing as a bridge between people, between goals, and between ideas. It is not just a matter of correctness and clarity, but of empathy, accessibility, and intentional design. I have developed in this course into a more assertive, strategic, and audience-aware writer, and I am now much more attuned to the ways that the words I choose can impact both business outcomes and personal impressions.

At Howard University, I have been repeatedly challenged to communicate with accuracy, professionalism, and pride. This course pushed me harder by requiring me to mold my communication to real-life applications: job searches, giving advice through online formats, and declaring my goals specifically. Each assignment wasn.t just an exercise it was a way of practicing how to think like a professional in the field, whether it was designing a survivor's guide to online spaces or crafting an intentional resume and LinkedIn profile.

One of the most enlightening pieces of writing for me was the Professional Purpose Memo. It forced me to step back and articulate what drives me, both academically and professionally. It helped me realize that technical writing is less about what I'm doing, but a way to demonstrate why I'm doing it. It forced me to align my aspirations with my values and helped me see the value in having a clear, consistent message when presenting myself in a professional setting.

The Job Application Package showed me the technical side of branding: organizing information for quick readability, choosing active verbs, and designing my documents to stand out in a professional manner. It wasn't so much about buzzwords as clarity, credibility, and confidence. I have learned now that a resume or LinkedIn profile is not so much a list it's a story, and I'm the author.

The Digital Survivor’s Guide enabled me to stretch both technical and creative muscles at the same time. It reminded me that technical writing needs to be not only clear but usable and accessible to a wide variety of people. Through organizational, tone, and layout choices, I was able to make a topic that could potentially be overwhelming into a helpful, even enjoyable, guide.

Finally, the fourth piece a project from another class allowed me to show how I apply technical communication to a broader context. It shows how writing can support research, enable collaboration, and broaden impact across disciplines.

In total, this portfolio reflects my growth as a communicator who values clarity, purpose, and impact. It's not just about being a better writer it's about being a better professional. I come to writing now knowing that every document can potentially connect, empower, and inform. These are the abilities that I will carry with me to internships, research, and eventually to my professional career. Technical writing has helped me hone my voice and my vision and now, I am excited about sharing that with the world.

1. Professional Purpose Memo

Assignment Summary & Commentary

Summary: This assignment required crafting a one-page Professional Purpose Memorandum (max 400 words) following standard memo formatting. The memo needed to articulate a clear professional purpose, identify target audiences, and outline actionable goals for the short-term (2 years), mid-term (5 years), and long-term (10-15 years), using concise and professional language.

Commentary & Learning: Completing this memo was a valuable exercise in self-reflection and strategic communication. It pushed me to define my professional identity, articulate my core values (like mentorship and promoting diversity in tech), and translate aspirations into concrete, time-bound goals. I practiced writing concisely, adhering to specific formatting requirements (memo structure), and maintaining a professional tone while minimizing first-person usage. This assignment reinforced the importance of audience awareness and having a clear narrative when presenting professional plans and purpose.

2. Job Application Package

Assignment Summary & Commentary

Summary: This assignment involved creating a comprehensive job application package tailored to a specific, real job description in my field. The package included the job description and link, a list of relevant coursework, a targeted résumé detailing experiences and skills, a professional cover letter addressed to the hiring manager, and a functional, field-specific LinkedIn profile.

Commentary & Learning: This project was crucial for understanding the practical application of technical communication in a job search. I learned the importance of analyzing a job description to tailor application materials effectively, using keywords and highlighting relevant experiences. Crafting the résumé emphasized clear, concise, action-oriented language and professional formatting. The cover letter provided practice in persuasive writing and adapting tone for a specific audience (the hiring manager). Developing the LinkedIn profile underscored the significance of maintaining a consistent and professional online presence, showcasing skills and experiences learned throughout my academic career.

3. Digital Survivor’s Guide

Assignment Summary & Commentary

Summary: The goal of this assignment was to create a technical "Survival Guide" compiling online communication resources specific to Computer Science. This included identifying and describing three research tools (like databases or foundational texts), three relevant professional societies, and three other useful internet sites, providing a 100-word synopsis for each. The guide required an executive summary, table of contents, list of figures, and an appendix listing job, internship, or scholarship resources, all formatted for usability by students and early-career professionals.

Commentary & Learning: This project honed my ability to research, evaluate, and curate relevant online resources for a specific audience (fellow CS students/professionals). I practiced technical writing by summarizing complex resources concisely (synopses, executive summary) and organizing information logically using elements like a table of contents. Making decisions about format and style to create a usable and effective document emphasized user-centered design principles. It also reinforced the importance of identifying credible and helpful resources for professional development and navigating the field.

4. Significant Course Project/Essay/Presentation

Assignment Summary & Commentary

Project Title: Cardio Sync (Bison Bytes Hackathon)

Course: Bison Bytes Hackathon (Extracurricular)

Summary: Cardio Sync is a web application developed during the Bison Bytes Hackathon to address cardiovascular health disparities, particularly in African American communities. It functions as a smart dashboard for doctors, enabling real-time monitoring of patient vitals (like SpO₂ and blood pressure). Using machine learning (Scikit-learn), it analyzes health trends to predict potential risks and sends proactive alerts, aiming to facilitate early intervention and improve patient outcomes in underserved populations.

Commentary & Learning: This project was significant as it allowed me to apply technical skills (Django, Python, JavaScript, ML) to a pressing real-world issue directly impacting my community. Collaborating under hackathon pressure honed teamwork and rapid development skills. Integrating backend logic with ML for predictive analysis and presenting complex health data clearly demonstrated my ability to bridge technical development with user-focused communication. Finishing 2nd in the Health Track validated the project's concept and execution, reinforcing the value of using technology for social impact.
